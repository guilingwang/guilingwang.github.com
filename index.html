Guiling Wang is the associate professor of Computer Science with a focus on Internet Computing, Cloud Computing and Service Computing. From 2007-2011 she is an assistant professor and post doctor in Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences (ICT, CAS). She obtained her Ph.D. in DCST of Tsinghua University in 2007. Her research interests include Information Integration, Software Integration, Services Composition and Mashup Technologies. She act as the principal investigator (PI) of National Natural Science Foundation of China(2010-2012), Post-Doctor Science Foundation of China in 2008, and a Changfeng Alliance SOA Strategic Study Project in 2008. She also acted as co-PI or main member of other NSFC,863,and collaboration project from industry. She has published some 30 scientific papers as conference- and journal- contributions. She has writen 2 academic books (1st author and 4th author). She has been authorized 2 patents. She is the PC member of SOCA'10, SOCA'09, ICSOC'12 Industry track, and EDOC SCDI'12 workshop etc.

Selected Projects

    Jan.2008 - Present End-User Service Programming Model for Supporting Web Information Resource Aggregation (PI)
    This project aimed at enabling end-users - those without programming skills - to extract and combine data from today‘s Web. We developed a prototype called Mashroom, consists of (1) a web server hosting a community of different forms of data sources, such as RSS feeds, RESTful services, SOAP based services; (2) Canvas, a mashup tool to combine data sources in a spreadsheet & workflow like manner; (3) Grubber, a tool enabling end users to build RESTful services from Web-based data sources. Compared with the pure flow-chart-centric mashup editors like Yahoo! Pipes, we believe Mashroom is better at balancing powerfulness and easiness-for-use for average end users. This work was supported by the Post-Doctor Science Foundation of China (Grant No. 20080430582).
    July.2008 - Present Zero-code Mobile Widget Programming & Management Environment, Collaborated with Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd. China (Co-PI)
    Based on pre-research of the end-user service programming and the Mashroom prototype, this project aimed at developing a "Zero-code" Programming Environment for the end-users to create a widget from web pages, RSS/Atom feeds, Open APIs, etc. This project also aimed at developing a mobile widget community for widget sharing and reusing.
    Oct.2008 - Jan. 2009 Changfeng Alliance SOA Strategic Study (PI)
    In this project, we aimed at making a SOA technology roadmap for Changfeng Alliance. We wrote the first research report on SOA technology in China.
